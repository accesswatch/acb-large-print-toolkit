## President’s Report

**April Board Meeting**  
**Jeff Bishop, President - BITS**

Before anything else, I would like us to pause for a moment and reflect on who we are and why we are here.

We are here for people.

At the heart of BITS is not simply technology, programs, committees, or systems. At the heart of BITS are people - people with hopes, needs, ideas, challenges, talents, and dreams. We are blessed with a tremendous amount of knowledge, experience, compassion, and ability within this organization. With that comes both opportunity and responsibility.

As we continue to grow, we must center our programs and services even more intentionally around what it means to be welcoming, to help members flourish, and to honor the small but meaningful things in life that matter most to people when we are made aware of them. This is about making sure that no one is left out, that people feel valued and respected, and that we do not lose sight of the simple human needs that can sometimes be overlooked.

There is a group within BITS actively working in this area, and I expect we will have more to share in the coming weeks. I believe this work is important, timely, and deeply aligned with who we strive to be as an organization.

I would also like to talk about leadership.

This message is directed first to myself, then to our Board of Directors, and also to our committee chairs, co-chairs, and committee members. Over the next couple of weeks, I will be reaching out to schedule conversations with each committee chair and co-chair, as well as with our officers. These conversations will be important, and I hope each of you will take some time to reflect before we meet.

In many recent conversations, I have noticed something that deserves our attention: some of the quieter and smaller voices in our organization are not being heard and valued as fully as they should be. For that, I take responsibility, and I am committed to putting a plan in place to address it. I cannot do that alone. I need your help, your honesty, and your partnership to make that effort meaningful and successful.

There is no “I” in team, and BITS is at its best when we remember that we are not just an organization - we are a family working toward the same shared goal: helping our membership flourish and doing what is best for the people we serve.

As you prepare for our conversations, I would ask each of you to reflect on two questions.

First: If you had three wishes for BITS, what would they be? These wishes might relate to the organization itself, to communication, to recognition, to your role, to leadership - including my own leadership - or to anything else that is personally on your mind. I encourage you to write these down and give yourself time to think. Do not rush. These conversations are meant to be thoughtful, respectful, and safe spaces for constructive reflection.

Second: What are two or three things about the organization that you would most like to see done differently?

In both cases, I ask that we approach these reflections constructively. Feelings and emotions are welcome, because they are real and they matter. At the same time, we must be careful to speak in ways that build up rather than tear down. The goal is not criticism for its own sake, but insight that helps us become stronger, more thoughtful, and more effective together.

I will be reaching out to each of you to schedule 30 to 60 minutes to talk, so please stay tuned for more information.

There is one more leadership point I want to raise, and that is the importance of making sure that our plates are not overflowing. I will be taking a careful look at current responsibilities across leadership and service areas, and it is possible that we may make some modest adjustments in how certain responsibilities are distributed. I do not say that to create concern. I say it because I want each of you to be as successful as possible in the areas where you are strongest and most energized.

This connects directly to shared responsibility.

Each of us has an area of focus, and each of us plays a role in shaping the experience of our members. Shared responsibility means that we work together, communicate openly, and support one another when demands become too heavy or when a different approach is needed. It means reaching across the dance floor, holding hands when needed, and making sure that each person has the support, encouragement, and flexibility they need to thrive.

Your voices are not optional in this process. They are essential. They are important, and they are vital to the future success of BITS.

Speaking of voices, let’s also not forget leaders that we must not worry about the small stuff. Remember, things will happen and we can always improve, so be kind in your interactions.

I want to emphasize that we are all busy and that comes with grace as well. It is for this reason I want to remind everyone that if you wish to hold a committee meeting, or any meeting of the minds, and you wish to use your personal Zoom meeting room then I am fine with this and the organization should support this. Meetings of committees that wish to continue to utilize BITS sponsored Zoom rooms are also fine. Be kind and let people be flexible. The only thing I would suggest is that presentations, board meetings, office hours and larger types of meeting places need to be in a BITS sponsored room please.

Now I would like to speak about innovation.

We have been working hard across many areas - programs, mentoring, fundraising, education, and organizational development. That work matters, and I am proud of it. But in the midst of doing so much, we must also be careful not to lose sight of simplicity.

Sometimes, in our efforts to build, grow, and serve, we can make things more complex than they need to be for new members, for long-time members, and even for leaders within the organization. Because of that, you will begin to see a significant effort around simplification: making it easier to join BITS, easier to renew, easier to engage with courses, easier to understand our calendar of activities, easier to access information, and easier to connect with the programs and services we offer.

We are always trying to make wise choices with the resources we have. We cannot do everything at once, even though we often try very hard. So I would simply ask that as we continue this work, you lead with grace. Offer ideas. Share constructive feedback. Assume good intentions. And know that when decisions are made, they are often made with real complexity in mind.

One of the highlights of my week came when a member reached out regarding the Android experience of BITS Connect and offered several thoughtful ways we might work around previously announced issues. That kind of communication is exactly what strengthens an organization. I will not name that individual here, but I do want to offer a public thank you. It was encouraging to see someone lead not only by identifying concern, but by bringing forward possible solutions. That spirit of engagement and care is deeply appreciated.

In closing, I return to where I began: people matter most.

Caring, leadership, shared responsibility, innovation, and constructive communication are not separate ideas. Together, they form the foundation of a stronger BITS. In the coming month, you will hear more about some meaningful changes and important efforts now underway. But beneath all of it is a simple truth: our emphasis on people is at the heart of our thinking.

To our leaders, I look forward to our conversations with openness, respect, and a genuine desire to learn from you. I want to better understand how I can support each of you - not only in your roles within BITS, but also as people whose lives, perspectives, and well-being matter.

Thank you for your leadership, your care, and your continued commitment to this organization and the people we serve.

Respectfully submitted,  
**Jeff Bishop**  
President  
**Blind Information Technology Solutions**

### Organizational Effectiveness Liaison Team Report

Date: April 8, 2026.

As Organizational Effectiveness Liaisons, our role is to observe patterns in communication, collaboration, and leadership dynamics across the organization, and to support conditions that enable all members to contribute effectively.

#### Observations

Across multiple committees and interactions, we are observing patterns that may be impacting overall organizational effectiveness:

-   Communication is sometimes experienced as directive or corrective in group settings
-   Some members appear hesitant to contribute or raise concerns openly
-   Decision-making authority is not always clearly defined, leading to overlap and tension
-   At times, individuals may work around one another rather than through shared processes in order to avoid conflict
-   In some instances, interactions have been experienced as discouraging or diminishing, particularly in public or group contexts

**Impact**

These patterns may be affecting several key areas:

-   Psychological safety within teams
-   Willingness to participate and share perspectives
-   Clarity of roles and responsibilities
-   Collaboration and coordination across committees

#### Strengths

We also observe strong commitment, initiative, and a shared desire among members to support the organization’s mission. These strengths provide a solid foundation for continued growth and improvement.

#### Recommendations

To support greater alignment and effectiveness, we suggest:

-   Clarifying roles, responsibilities, and decision-making boundaries across committees
-   Establishing shared guidelines for communication, particularly in group settings
-   Encouraging space for all voices to be heard without interruption or immediate correction
-   Supporting feedback processes that emphasize respect, learning, and constructive engagement
-   Reinforcing shared accountability for how communication occurs, in addition to the outcomes of decisions

This report is offered in support of strengthening collaboration, clarity, and overall organizational effectiveness.

Respectfully submitted,

Thursday July 16

10:00 AM

Smart Living Stronger Access Appliances Community and Advocacy

Be part of a conversation about appliances and accessibility, and why inclusive design must extend into every part of daily life. Our presentation will highlight the launch of the Appliance Portal, efforts to connect with the community through YouTube, and the importance of bringing greater awareness to vendors, advocates, and consumers alike. By sharing experiences, building visibility, and speaking with a stronger collective voice, we can help drive meaningful progress in accessibility and advocacy.

1:00 PM

Capturing The World Through Photography With Confidence

Many of us carry smartphones or wearable devices with built-in cameras, but if you are blind or visually impaired, using them may feel unfamiliar or even intimidating. This session is designed for smart device beginners or intermediate users and will start with the basics of how to take photos using accessible features. We will explore simple techniques to help you understand what’s in your pictures using description tools and apps. By the end, you’ll feel more confident capturing and sharing moments in ways that work for you.

Friday July 17

10:00 AM

What’s new with the Monarch

Come learn about the Monarch multi-line Braille display from the American Printing house for the blind. Discover all about tactile graphics, Monarch apps, and the Monarch RISE project.

1:00 PM

Microsoft Narrator A Tool For Making The Transition From Low Vision To No Vision

If Using screen magnification and a mouse with your Windows PC is no longer working effectively for you due to ongoing vision loss, there is an available alternative. Microsoft Narrator, a free screen reader built into the Windows operating system, can enable you to read the contents displayed on your computer’s screen, as well as hear the text in documents, email, and webpages, as well as any other content on your computer screen. In this presentation, we will show you how to put this powerful tool to work for you, and help you maintain your independence, as your vision continues to Transition From Low Vision To No Vision.

Saturday July 18

10:00 AM

Trends in AI and making it accessible

Blind Information Technology Solutions, along with a panel of experts, will explore the latest trends in AI from a blind and low vision perspective. The session will highlight how tools like ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, Claude, and Accessibility Agents are expanding access, creativity, and innovation. Attendees will learn how blind and low vision people are not only using these technologies, but also helping shape what comes next. It will be a practical and engaging look at how accessible AI is changing the world around us.

1:00 PM

Accessible blogging with Substack

Substack is an accessible web-based platform designed to allow you to unleash your imagination for creative writing. In this presentation, we will explore this exciting platform, and hear from 3 users, who will share their experiences using Substack as a creative way to reach a wide audience.

#### Member Feedback and Engagement

The IT Committee would like to begin by expressing our sincere appreciation to all members who have taken the time to submit feedback through the BITS website feedback form. Your input is essential in helping us improve our technology, services, and overall member experience.

To ensure we can respond effectively and efficiently, we kindly ask members to keep the following in mind when submitting feedback:

-   This is the place to provide your feedback honestly and anonymously.
-   **Include Contact Information**  
    If you are seeking a response, please provide an email address and/or phone number so we can follow up directly.
-   **Provide Clear and Detailed Information**  
    When reporting an issue or sharing feedback, please be as specific as possible. For example:
    -   Describe how you navigated to the page
    -   Identify where on the page the issue occurred
    -   Explain what you expected versus what actually happened

Clear, detailed feedback allows our team to investigate and resolve issues more efficiently.
