Privacy: what happens to my audio?

Your audio file is uploaded to this server, transcribed using a local copy of the Whisper model, and then deleted immediately once the transcript is sent back to you. The audio is never forwarded to OpenAI, any cloud API, or any third party. Transcription happens entirely in this server's memory using the faster-whisper library.

The transcript itself is also deleted after download. Nothing is stored.

What is Whisper medium? How accurate is it?

Whisper is an open-source speech recognition model released by OpenAI in 2022. BITS Whisperer uses the medium size model (1.5 GB, 300M parameters), which gives the best balance of accuracy and speed for CPU inference.

Accuracy depends on audio quality. For clear speech in a quiet room, Whisper medium produces near-human-level transcription in English and performs well in over 50 other languages. Background noise, multiple speakers talking at the same time, strong accents, and technical jargon reduce accuracy.

The model runs with int8 quantization via CTranslate2, which is 4x faster than standard float32 inference with no meaningful accuracy loss.

Tips for getting the best transcript
  • One speaker at a time. Whisper does not separate overlapping voices. Recordings where people talk over each other will produce garbled output for those sections.
  • Minimize background noise. Music, HVAC hum, and crowd noise all reduce accuracy. Close-mic recordings (headset, lapel mic, or phone held near the speaker) work best.
  • Specify the language for short clips. Auto-detect uses the first 30 seconds of audio to identify the language -- a very short clip may be misidentified.
  • Review the transcript. Whisper is accurate but not perfect. Proper nouns, acronyms (like "BITS" or "ACB"), and technical terms may need correction.
  • Use Markdown output first. Download the .md file, clean it up in a text editor, then use the Convert tab to turn it into an accessible web page, PDF, or Word document. This two-step approach gives you full control over the final result.
What file formats are supported?
  • MP3 (.mp3) -- most common audio format; works well
  • WAV (.wav) -- uncompressed; highest quality but large files
  • M4A (.m4a) -- Apple audio format from iPhone recordings and Voice Memos
  • OGG (.ogg) -- open-source compressed audio
  • FLAC (.flac) -- lossless compression; good quality, smaller than WAV
  • AAC (.aac) -- compressed audio used by many devices
  • Opus (.opus) -- modern codec used by voice calls and video conferences

Video files (MP4, MOV, WebM) are not accepted directly. Extract the audio track first using a tool like VLC or ffmpeg, then upload the audio file.
