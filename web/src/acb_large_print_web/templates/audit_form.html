What does the audit do?

The audit checks your Word document (.docx) against the ACB Large Print Guidelines and WCAG 2.2 AA requirements. It examines fonts, sizes, spacing, alignment, emphasis, headings, margins, and document properties. You receive a detailed report showing each finding with its severity and the relevant guideline reference.

Understanding severity levels
Critical
Issues that make the document unreadable for low-vision users. Must be fixed. Examples: wrong font, text smaller than 18pt, italic formatting.
High
Significant readability or structure issues. Should be fixed. Examples: wrong heading sizes, justified alignment, bold misused for body emphasis, skipped heading levels.
Medium
Compliance gaps that affect quality but may not block readability. Examples: incorrect line spacing, missing page numbers, no document title.
Low
Minor issues or best-practice recommendations. Example: widow and orphan control not enabled.
