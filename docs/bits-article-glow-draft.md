Most of us at ACB have opened a flyer, a meeting agenda, or a chapter newsletter and thought, "I cannot read this." The print is too small. The font is wrong. The colors fight each other. The list of links says "click here" over and over with no clue where any of them lead.

The frustrating part is that the people who made those documents almost always meant well. They just did not know what large print really requires, or how a screen reader will hear the words on the page. They are missing tools, not goodwill.

That is the gap GLOW is here to close.

## What GLOW is

GLOW is a free, web-based toolkit from ACB's Community Access project. You open it in your browser at glow.acb.org. You drop in a document, and GLOW tells you in plain English what is working, what is not, and what to do next. In many cases, GLOW will also fix the document for you.

The name stands for Guidelines for Large-print and Online Writing. The "large print" part follows the ACB Board of Publications Large Print Guidelines, revised in May 2025. The "online" part follows the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines, version 2.2, level AA, which is the worldwide standard for digital accessibility.

You do not have to know any of those names to use it. GLOW knows them for you.

## What you can do with it

There are four things most people will use GLOW for.

**Check a document.** Upload a Word file, a PowerPoint, an Excel sheet, or a PDF. GLOW reads it and gives you back a friendly report. It says things like "Your headings skip from Heading 1 to Heading 4, which makes screen readers lose their place," or "Three of your pictures are missing alt text." Every finding includes a short fix you can do yourself.

**Fix a document.** For most issues, GLOW will do the work for you. Click the Fix tab, upload the same file, and download a corrected version. Heading styles get repaired, alt text placeholders are added where they were missing, table headers are marked properly, and fonts are nudged toward the ACB large print recommendation. You stay in control. Nothing is changed without your file going back to you.

**Convert a document.** GLOW can turn a Word file into accessible HTML for your chapter website. It can turn a PDF into Markdown for reuse. It can turn a Markdown file into a clean Word document. It can also produce a print-ready PDF that already follows the ACB large print rules. Several of the most common requests we get from affiliates ("can I get this in large print?", "can I get this on the website?") can be answered in about a minute.

**Build a template.** Starting from scratch? GLOW gives you a blank Word template, a blank HTML page, or a blank Markdown file that already has the ACB large print settings baked in. Open it, type your content, and you are done.

## Why this matters for affiliates

ACB affiliates put out a lot of material. Meeting notices. Annual reports. Election ballots. Convention programs. Workshops. Membership renewal forms. Social posts. Behind every one of those is usually a single volunteer with a copy of Word and a deadline.

That volunteer should not also have to memorize the ACB large print spec, or learn WCAG, or know how Microsoft's Accessibility Checker differs from Adobe's. They should be able to write the document, hand it to a tool, and have the tool catch the things they missed.

That is the day-to-day promise of GLOW. It gives every affiliate the same starting point, regardless of staff size or technical experience. A two-person chapter in a small town gets the same checks the national office gets.

It also helps with consistency. When every affiliate publishes documents that follow the same large print guidelines, ACB looks like one organization. Members who travel between chapters, or who get materials from more than one affiliate, recognize the shared style. The brand becomes part of the accessibility.

## Why this matters for everyday users

If you are not the person making the document, GLOW still helps you.

Got a PDF from a doctor's office that you cannot read? Drop it into GLOW's Convert tool. You can get it back as plain text, as accessible HTML you can zoom in your browser, or as a clean Word file you can resize on your own.

Got a long article a friend sent you? GLOW has a Document Chat that lets you ask questions about the content out loud, including "summarize this in plain language" or "read me just the dates and times."

Got a picture you need to describe? GLOW has an Alt-Text Helper that will draft several options for you in different styles, so you can pick the one that fits.

None of this requires an account. None of it costs anything. None of it is stored on our servers after you close the tab.

## A note on privacy

We take this seriously. Your uploads are processed and then deleted. We do not look at your documents. We do not train any AI on them. When AI is used at all (for example, in Document Chat or Alt-Text Helper), the request is sanitized for personal information first, and you can turn AI features off entirely. The full privacy policy is linked from every page in the footer.

## How to start

1. Open glow.acb.org in any modern browser, on a computer, tablet, or phone.
2. Pick the tool that matches what you want to do: Audit, Fix, Convert, Template, Alt-Text Helper, or Document Chat.
3. Drop in your file. Read the friendly results. Download what you need.

That is it. There is no installation, no login, no waiting list.

## Help shape what comes next

GLOW is built by people who use screen readers, magnification, and large print every day. We want to hear what is working for you and what is not. Every page has a Feedback button at the top. If you have ideas for what would help your affiliate or your own reading life, please send them. We read every one.

Large print is a civil right. Digital accessibility is a civil right. GLOW is one way ACB is making both of those rights easier to deliver, one document at a time.
