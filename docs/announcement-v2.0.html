GLOW 2.0 Press Release

GLOW (Guided Layout & Output Workflow) 2.0 Launches: Accessibility Workflows That Move from Friction to Flow

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE – April 17, 2026

The Blind Information Technology Solutions (BITS) community today announced GLOW (Guided Layout & Output Workflow) Accessibility Toolkit 2.0, a major release that moves teams from one-step document checking to full accessibility production workflows: upload, triage, fix, convert, interrogate, and publish.

GLOW 2.0 is available at glow.bits-acb.org.

GLOW 2.0 will be released on or around 8:00 PM on Saturday, April 18, 2026.

“From what I experienced, GLOW does not just give me information that creates extra work for me to do in order to get accessible documents out the door. It actually automates work I probably was not doing all that well in the first place. In other words, it is giving me a way to improve my product, and it is also making my work easier and quicker. Getting both quality and quantity benefits from the same product is not something I have experienced that often, and it is actually pretty cool.”

A Real-World Story

A chapter coordinator opens an agenda thirty minutes before a meeting. It is not large print compliant. One board member needs a web version, another needs a print-ready copy, and someone else asks for a plain-language summary.

Before GLOW 2.0, this often ended in delay or compromise. With GLOW 2.0, teams can upload once, choose the right path, and complete accessible output under real deadlines.

What Is New in GLOW 2.0

1. Quick Start Guided Path

The biggest change in GLOW 2.0 is simple: people no longer have to guess where to begin.

Quick Start meets users where they are. You upload your file, and GLOW points you to the best next step. No tool hunting. No “which tab do I use?” confusion. Just a clear path forward.

Think of the volunteer who opens a document right before a meeting and needs help now, not in twenty minutes. Quick Start is designed for that moment.

“The new quick start feature is yet another example of innovation that continues to bring people first capabilities to users,” said Jeff Bishop, President, Blind Information Technology Solutions (BITS).

2. Agentic Document Chat with Tool Calling

Document Chat turns accessibility from a checklist into a conversation.

Instead of asking, “Where do I click next?” users can ask what they really need:

  • “What should I fix first?”
  • “Show me where my heading structure is broken.”
  • “Can this be fixed automatically, or does it need human review?”
  • “Explain this rule in plain English so I can teach my team.”

GLOW answers with context from the document itself, not generic advice. It feels like having a calm accessibility coach sitting beside you, helping you move from uncertainty to action.

Conversation history is organized in clear turns and can be exported for handoffs, training, and compliance records.

“Getting a document to a final state of excellence is difficult,” said Jeff Bishop, President, Blind Information Technology Solutions (BITS). “Bringing this tool to the masses does not just address low-vision gaps. It brings clarity and consistency to the process of creating professional-looking documents that meet the needs of everyone who consumes them.”

“We want content creators to put their best foot forward when they present their creations to the world, and GLOW shines a light on this gap and brings people out of the darkness and into the light with greater clarity,” Bishop added.

3. BITS Whisperer (Audio Transcription)

BITS Whisperer helps users turn spoken content into usable written content in minutes.

If a chapter records a meeting, an interview, or a training session, they can now bring that audio into GLOW and leave with clean, editable output they can publish, review, and improve.

This is especially powerful for teams with limited time: less retyping, less delay, faster access to information.

“We wanted transcription to be practical for chapter work, not a separate technical project,” said Jeff Bishop, President of Blind Information Technology Solutions (BITS). “BITS Whisperer gives teams a real-world way to move from recorded content to accessible written content in one guided path.”

4. Consent and Privacy Gate

GLOW now starts with trust.

Before people begin, they see clear, plain-language consent explaining how the platform handles data. There are no hidden surprises and no confusing policy walls.

Users can make an informed choice, continue with confidence, and revisit that choice at any time.

5. MarkItDown 0.2+ Integration

GLOW now accepts more of the real-world content people actually work with every day.

That means fewer dead ends, fewer “unsupported format” moments, and more progress from a single platform. Teams can bring in what they have and keep moving.

The result is simple: less friction at the door, more time spent improving content.

6. Vision-Ready OCR Path for Scanned Content

Some of the hardest documents are the ones that look readable but are actually locked in scans and images.

GLOW 2.0 is built to handle more of those difficult cases, helping teams recover meaning from image-heavy content and bring it back into an editable, accessible workflow.

For end users, this means fewer “we cannot do anything with this file” moments and more “we can work with this” outcomes.

7. Reliable Performance When Everyone Needs Help at Once

When demand spikes, GLOW keeps the experience steady instead of slowing to a crawl.

In plain terms: if many heavy jobs arrive at the same moment, GLOW protects people who are already working and gives clear guidance to those waiting, so no one is left wondering if the app is broken.

What users can expect:

  • More stable performance during busy periods
  • Clear “please retry shortly” messages instead of confusing failures
  • Better odds that urgent tasks finish cleanly the first time

Think of a deadline hour with multiple chapters uploading at once. Instead of random timeouts, users get predictable behavior and a clear path forward.

Flashback: How We Got Here

GLOW began as a practical response to a common reality: accessibility issues were often discovered at the end of publication workflows, when fixes were expensive and stressful.

Earlier releases focused on foundational capabilities:

  • high-signal audits
  • guided Word remediation
  • standards-profile workflows
  • template generation
  • conversion and export
  • explicit privacy behavior

GLOW 2.0 builds on that foundation with guided entry points, local AI assistance, audio-to-text workflows, and agentic content interrogation.

In short, the product moved from “find issues” to “help teams finish accessible output on time.”

Why This Matters to Chapters and Affiliates

For these users, accessibility is not abstract. It is meeting notices, election materials, newsletters, and board packets that must be usable by real people, on real timelines.

GLOW 2.0 is designed for that operational reality:

  • no forced account setup
  • browser-first experience
  • practical defaults for non-technical users
  • advanced controls for expert teams
  • local processing pathways for privacy-sensitive work

“Simplicity is crucial when we bring experiences to life for users,” said Deb Lewis, President of the American Council of the Blind. “BITS is evolving GLOW into a truly usable platform and meeting people where they are in their digital journeys.”

Looking Ahead: July 2026 Desktop Expansion

BITS plans additional major feature work in July for desktop experiences, including:

  • desktop-based BITS Whisperer for Windows
  • desktop-based BITS Whisperer for Mac
  • additional GLOW desktop implementations as time permits

This roadmap continues the same 2.0 design principles: practical workflows, local-first privacy, and accessibility by default.
