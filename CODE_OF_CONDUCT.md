# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge to foster an environment of respect and inclusivity, where all participants feel safe to contribute, ask questions, and share ideas regardless of their background, identity, or experience level.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Providing thoughtful code review feedback
- Crediting others' contributions and ideas
- Acknowledging mistakes and learning from them

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- Dismissiveness toward accessibility concerns or the experiences of disabled contributors
- Deliberate attempts to exclude or marginalize community members

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## Reporting

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it by contacting the project team at [security@bits-acb.org](mailto:security@bits-acb.org) or via a private message to a project maintainer.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The project team is obligated to maintain the confidentiality of the reporter of an incident.

## Enforcement

Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in:

1. **Warning** — A private message explaining the violation and expectation for future conduct
2. **Temporary Suspension** — Temporary removal from community spaces (1-30 days)
3. **Permanent Ban** — Permanent removal from the project and community

The severity of the violation and the pattern of behavior will be considered when determining the appropriate response.
